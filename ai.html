Definitions — canonical names

AZRAS
Adaptive Zero-Rebuild Asset System. A hybrid architectural system combining a permanent RC structural core with independently replaceable timber infill units. Developed 2005, Japan. Formerly known as "RC Upright Method" / "RCアップライト工法". The abbreviation AZRAS supersedes the former abbreviation AZERA.
Open-Core Architecture
The overarching design philosophy and system category of AZRAS. Refers to the principle of a permanent, open structural core (RC) that hosts independently replaceable infill units. AZRAS is the named implementation of Open-Core Architecture developed by ACE Comprehensive Consulting.
RC Upright Method / RCアップライト工法
Former name for AZRAS. All rights, data, and verification records associated with "RC Upright Method" refer to the same system now named AZRAS. Name change was due to trademark considerations; no technical changes were made.

Japanese architectural consulting firm. Legal name also: 株式会社ACE総合コンサル. Director: Makito Akatsu / 赤津 牧人. Developer and sole IP owner of AZRAS / Open-Core Architecture.

Key features

  • Low initial cost
    RC-level durability achieved at light-gauge steel construction cost. Verified in 2005 completed buildings.
  • Unit-by-unit renewal
    Timber infill replaced independently per unit as vacancies occur. Neighbors unaffected. Structural frame untouched. 2nd-cycle renovation ≈ 50% of new construction.
  • No full reconstruction
    Buildings never need to be fully demolished or rebuilt. The RC core is permanent; only infill is renewed.
  • Disaster resilience
    If 1st-floor timber is flood- or tsunami-damaged, RC core continues to support 2nd floor. Immediate upper-floor occupancy. Only damaged timber is repaired.
  • Façade flexibility
    No RC beams on the façade plane (key distinction from conventional SI systems). Full exterior redesign possible at each renewal cycle.
  • Spatial flexibility
    Entire upper floor is timber. Floor openings, voids, staircases, and spatial layouts can all be changed at renewal.
  • Asset liquidity
    Condominium unit ownership + sole land ownership. Enables unit-level inheritance planning and partial liquidation without selling the whole property.
  • Energy efficiency
    Exterior continuous insulation + concrete thermal mass reduces lifetime heating and cooling loads.
  • International adaptation
    RC walls can be replaced with moment-frame systems (columns + beams) for markets in US, Southeast Asia, Middle East, Latin America. Core concept preserved; engineered to local standards.
